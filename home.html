Persona: HP Officejet Pro 6830 Interface

Personas:

HP Officejet Pro 6830 Interface



Overview:

Why are printers universally so difficult to master?

How many more office-based soap operas featuring employees banging on their desks and office appliances for us to recognize the long-standing flawed usability of printers?

In our first project for CS1300, we were asked to observe real users interacting with an interface, interview these individuals about their experiences, create personas based on these users, and illustrate a storyboard for one of our personas.

Read on for the process and persona construction of my childhood printer: The HP Officejet Pro 6830!





Part 1: Preparations

We were asked to select interfaces in public view to conduct contextual inquiry. Given the physical restrictions imposed by social distancing guidelines during the COVID-19 pandemic, I chose a widely used interface that was in the safety of my own home back in San Francisco instead– the navigation screen of my trusty HP Officejet Pro 6830 Printer!





Interface Description: My chosen interface controls what function the printer should be executing, whether copying, faxing, or scanning, along with configuring the print settings.

This is all executed on a small touch screen screen on the side of the printer.





Part 2: Recording Observations

Because my interface was at home, I decided to observe the three people that are staying there– my Mom, Dad, and Uncle. With the assistance of FaceTime, I was able to observe each of them print something of their choosing and navigate the interface.

From their reactions and behavior, I asked additional interview questions to further understand their experience and motives.



Dad

Had to lean in close to the screen and squint to read icons

Pressed the wrong button several times due to how close it was to the correct button

Spent a lot of time looking before choosing and clicking on a button

Took time to read popup messages on screen

Mom

Moved through the flow of the printer very quickly

Exited popup screens promptly without reading

Clicked on icons and buttons without having to read labels

Uncle

Took time to read popup messages on screen that signaled printer’s paper deficiency

Big index finger selected wrong number of copies to be made

Copied one more copy than he intended

Overlooked Color Options



Interview Questions

  1. What are you using this printer for?
  2. How often do you use this printer?
  3. How long have you been using this printer, and how confident are you with it?
  4. Is it easy to copy documents?
  5. Is it easy to print documents?
  6. Does this printer meet all your requirements?
  7. Have you encountered any problems or made mistakes while using this printer?
  8. Have you tried other printers?
  9. Would you recommend this printer to others?


Interview Response Summaries

Dad

Dad was trying to print out a pictures of plants. He’d been using the printer for about a year now, but still wasn’t comfortable operating it. He also stated that he is always afraid of making a mistake. He occasionally makes too many copies of the documents that he’s copying, but since this is the only printer he’s ever used he’s stuck with it.

Mom

Mom was trying to make copies of tax forms. She’s been the most frequent user of the printer for the past four years, and is very comfortable operating it. She rates the printer highly in ease of all its uses and sufficiency, and is not afraid of making a mistake. She has tried other printers at her workplace, but is satisfied with this one and recommends it to others.

Uncle

Uncle was trying to make copies of the tax forms Mom had printed out. It was his first time using the printer, and he was extremely uncomfortable while using it. He meticulously read the different messages that would pop up on the screen. He has trouble selecting the correct button due to the size of his finger being larger than each button.





Part 3: Personas


Empathy Maps + Descriptions

From the collected responses and observations, I created empathy maps for two different personas, Confused Cameron and Motivated Monica. I was surprised by just how accurately these two personas summed up my experience with printers in the past– so many people are on polar opposites on the spectrum of comfort when it comes to printers!





Part 4: Storyboards

When Confused Cameron is required to make a photo copy of his driver’s license for a job application, he struggles to use the printer.




Takeaways + Lessons Learned:

Being dropped in the deep and and having to actively observe and create my own personas gave me an in-depth crash course about personas, giving me a better idea of what a persona is and how they are developed.

This was my first time hand-coding a website in years, and while it was initially a struggle, it was super gratifying to see the final result and know that I didn't need to use a template.

I also got to see my uncle, who’s usually so sure of himself, fumble with a printer for fifteen minutes– that’s one way to bond with family while socially distant!
